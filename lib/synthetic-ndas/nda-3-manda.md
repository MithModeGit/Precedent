MUTUAL CONFIDENTIALITY AGREEMENT

This Mutual Confidentiality Agreement (this "Agreement") is entered into as of [DATE] (the "Effective Date") by and between Northfield Capital Group, LLC, a Delaware limited liability company ("Party A"), and Cascade Systems, Inc., a Delaware corporation ("Party B") (each, a "Party" and together, the "Parties").

RECITALS

The Parties are considering a potential transaction or strategic relationship, including without limitation a possible acquisition, merger, investment, or other business combination involving Party B (the "Transaction"). In connection with evaluating the Transaction, the Parties may disclose to each other certain non-public, confidential, and proprietary information. This Agreement sets forth the Parties' obligations with respect to the use and protection of such information.

1. DEFINITION OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

"Confidential Information" means any and all non-public information, in any form or medium, disclosed by one Party (the "Disclosing Party") to the other Party (the "Receiving Party") in connection with the evaluation of the Transaction, including without limitation: financial statements, projections, business plans, customer and supplier information, personnel information, intellectual property, technical specifications, trade secrets, market analyses, and any notes, summaries, analyses, compilations, or other materials prepared by or for the Receiving Party that contain or reflect such information.

Confidential Information includes the existence of this Agreement, the fact that discussions are occurring between the Parties, and the terms of any proposed Transaction.

2. EXCLUSIONS FROM CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

Confidential Information shall not include information that:

(a) is or becomes publicly available other than through a breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party;

(b) was already known to the Receiving Party at the time of disclosure, as evidenced by the Receiving Party's written records predating the disclosure;

(c) is received by the Receiving Party from a third party who is under no obligation of confidentiality with respect to such information; or

(d) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without reference to the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information, as evidenced by the Receiving Party's written records.

3. OBLIGATIONS OF THE RECEIVING PARTY

The Receiving Party agrees to: (a) maintain the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information in strict confidence using no less than the same degree of care the Receiving Party applies to its own confidential information of like sensitivity; (b) use Confidential Information solely for evaluating the Transaction; and (c) not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without the Disclosing Party's prior written consent, except as expressly permitted under this Agreement.

4. PERMITTED DISCLOSURES TO REPRESENTATIVES

Each Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information to its directors, officers, employees, legal counsel, financial advisors, and accountants who need to know such information for purposes of evaluating the Transaction ("Representatives"). Each Receiving Party shall be responsible for ensuring that its Representatives maintain the confidentiality of the Confidential Information disclosed to them and shall be liable for any breach of this Agreement by its Representatives.

5. RESIDUALS

Notwithstanding any other provision of this Agreement, each Receiving Party and its Representatives shall be free to use any Residual Information. "Residual Information" means any information retained in the unaided memory of the Receiving Party's Representatives who have had access to Confidential Information, including ideas, concepts, know-how, and techniques contained therein, provided that such Representative's primary purpose in retaining such information is not to circumvent this Agreement.

6. COMPELLED DISCLOSURE

If the Receiving Party is required by applicable law, regulation, or valid legal process to disclose any Confidential Information, the Receiving Party shall, to the extent legally permitted, provide the Disclosing Party with prompt written notice and cooperate with the Disclosing Party in seeking a protective order or other appropriate relief prior to making any disclosure. In the absence of such relief, the Receiving Party may disclose only the minimum Confidential Information required to comply with such legal obligation.

7. TERM

The obligations under this Agreement shall apply to Confidential Information disclosed during the period beginning on the Effective Date and ending twenty-four (24) months after the Effective Date (the "Disclosure Period"). Obligations of confidentiality with respect to Confidential Information disclosed during the Disclosure Period shall continue for a period of three (3) years following the date of disclosure.

8. RETURN OR DESTRUCTION

Upon the Disclosing Party's written request or upon termination of this Agreement, the Receiving Party shall promptly return or destroy all Confidential Information, including all copies and derivatives, and certify in writing to the Disclosing Party that it has done so; provided, however, that each Receiving Party may retain one archival copy for legal and compliance purposes, subject to the ongoing confidentiality obligations of this Agreement.

9. REMEDIES

The Parties acknowledge that any breach of this Agreement may cause irreparable harm and that the non-breaching Party may be entitled to seek equitable relief, including injunction and specific performance, in addition to all other remedies available under applicable law, provided that the non-breaching Party shall post such bond or other security as may be required by a court of competent jurisdiction as a condition for the granting of such relief.

10. NO OBLIGATION TO PROCEED

Nothing in this Agreement shall obligate either Party to proceed with any Transaction or to continue discussions. Either Party may terminate discussions at any time without liability to the other Party, except as expressly set forth herein.

11. GOVERNING LAW AND JURISDICTION

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of New York. Each Party hereby consents to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of the State of New York and the United States District Court for the Southern District of New York in connection with any dispute arising out of or relating to this Agreement.

12. ENTIRE AGREEMENT

This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties with respect to the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior discussions, negotiations, and agreements relating thereto.

13. AMENDMENT AND WAIVER

This Agreement may be amended only by a written instrument signed by both Parties. No waiver of any provision shall be effective unless in writing, and no waiver shall be deemed a continuing waiver.

14. ASSIGNMENT

Neither Party may assign this Agreement or any rights hereunder without the other Party's prior written consent, except in connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of all or substantially all of such Party's assets.

15. COUNTERPARTS

This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original. Electronic signatures shall be deemed valid and binding.
